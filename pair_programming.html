Pairing and skill diversity variations

Expert-Expert
Defined as: Both partners have a similar level of expertise in both the tools and domain
Strengths: - High productivity - Can lead to more efficiant and higher quality work. - Allows for both developers to spur off and explore different appraoches to solving the problem when at a decision making crossroad.
Weaknesses: - Rarely leads to new-innovative approaches to problems since both parties are less likely to question the norms of the framework or codebase
Additional Groundrules: - RESPECT - The pair *must* take turns driving vs navigating
Novice-Expert
Defined as: The partners have different levels of experteese in either the tools or domain.
Strengths: - A novice is more likely to question patterns and norms. - The expert is more likely to question established patterns when they have to explain those patterns to the novice - Provides opportunity for learning about the tools or domain for the novice. - Forces the expert to slow down and produce better quality work.
Weaknesses: - Extra caution and empathy is required of the expert to ensure that the mentor doesn't end up passivly watching. - Can feel slower for the expert.
Additional Groundrules: - The novice must be driving for the bulk of the time. - The expert SHOULD NOT do side research. Instead the expert should tell the novice where to look if documents are needed for reference.
Novice-Novice
Defined as: The partners have similar levels of experteese in both tools and domain and that experteese is limited. This typically refers to interns/students and new hires.
Strengths: - Will significantly increase the capabilities of the two for solving problems outside of their existing skill set. - Prevents the novices from feeling stuck or trapped in a task outside of their skill set. - Allows for time for the expert to have a mental break or work on other tasks as needed.
Weaknesses: - The resulting work needs careful review. - Can reinforce bad habbits without an expert present asynchronously.
Additional Groundrules: - An expert must be present asynchronously and available for review + asking question. - The novices must take turns driving - Empathy is required of both novices to prevent one from passivly watching.
Mob
Strengths: - Very effective when approaching large architectural issues in a codebase everyone is familiar with. - Can be great for dealing with OnFire bugs üî• - Can be a great tool from team building
Weaknesses: - Will become a garbage-fire if effective communication patterns and channels are not identified üî•üî•üî• - Requires empathy to ensure that novices don't become passive watchers.
Additional Groundrules: - Teams should determin communication patterns and channels in advance. - Should be done in person or via video share - The most novice members of the group should drive.
